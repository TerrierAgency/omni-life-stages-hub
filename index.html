Stage 1

Puppy Stage: 0 – 12 Months

The most rapid period of growth in your dog's life. Getting nutrition right now sets the foundation for everything.

What puppies need

Puppies grow at an extraordinary rate — some breeds increase their birth weight by 40-50 times in the first year. This requires nutrient-dense food that supports:

  • Skeletal growth — calcium and phosphorus in the correct 1.2:1 ratio
  • Brain development — DHA from Omega-3 for cognitive function and trainability
  • Immune building — antioxidants, vitamins E and C during the vaccination period
  • Digestive maturation — prebiotics to establish healthy gut flora
[Image: Puppy eating from a bowl, healthy and energetic]

Key milestones

Puppy Timeline

  • 6 – 16 weeks: Primary vaccination period — immune support is critical
  • 3 – 6 months: Teething — soft food can help with discomfort
  • 6 months: Switch from 3-4 meals to 2 meals per day
  • 10 – 12 months: Begin transition to adult food

Common concerns

Sensitive tummies — Puppies' digestive systems are still developing. Sudden diet changes can easily cause upset.

Skin developing — The skin barrier isn't fully mature, making them more susceptible to dryness and irritation.

Growth-related joint stress — Large breeds are particularly prone to developmental joint issues if nutrition isn't balanced.

Stage 2

Adult Stage: 1 – 7 Years

Your dog's prime years. The focus shifts from growth to maintaining optimal health, managing weight, and preventing early decline.

[Image: Adult dog running in a park, healthy coat, full of energy]

Three pillars of adult health

  • Weight management — Over 50% of UK dogs are overweight. Maintaining a healthy weight reduces risk of diabetes, heart disease, and joint problems
  • Skin and coat — Omega-3 and 6 fatty acids are essential for skin barrier function and a shiny, healthy coat
  • Digestive balance — A healthy gut microbiome supports immunity, nutrient absorption, and overall wellbeing

Common health issues

Skin irritation — Allergies often first appear between ages 1-3. Itching, redness, and hot spots are common.

Early joint wear — Even in young adults, repetitive exercise and genetics can start to affect cartilage.

Digestive sensitivity — Food intolerances and inflammatory conditions can develop during adulthood.

[Video: Keeping your adult dog in peak condition]

Signs to watch for

Adjust portions based on body condition — you should be able to feel your dog's ribs without pressing hard, and they should have a visible waist from above.

  • Persistent scratching, licking, or chewing at skin
  • Unexplained weight gain or loss
  • Dull, dry coat or excessive shedding
  • Stiffness after exercise or reluctance to play

Stage 3

Senior Stage: 7+ Years

As your dog ages, their body changes. Targeted nutrition and supplementation helps them stay comfortable, mobile, and mentally sharp.

What changes

Senior dogs experience a 20-30% reduction in metabolic rate. They need fewer calories but higher concentrations of specific nutrients.

  • Arthritis and stiffness — the most common condition, affecting mobility and quality of life
  • Reduced immunity — slower healing, more susceptible to infections
  • Coat and skin changes — less oil production, thinner, drier coat
  • Cognitive decline — affects up to 68% of dogs over 15
[Image: Senior dog resting comfortably, greying muzzle, gentle expression]

How to adapt their diet

  • Increase Omega-3 for anti-inflammatory support
  • Add glucosamine and MSM for joint maintenance
  • Include antioxidants (vitamins E and C) to combat oxidative stress
  • Feed smaller, more frequent meals to ease digestion

When to talk to your vet

  • Struggling to get up or obvious pain when moving
  • Significant appetite changes
  • Unexplained weight loss or muscle wasting
  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Confusion, disorientation, or sleep changes
